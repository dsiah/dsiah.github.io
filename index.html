There are many algorithms for generating mazes. Eller's Maze generation algorithm is a popular algorithm that produces a low-bias maze in efficient time and space.

Encoding Mazes

Before we discuss the algorithm, let's take a step back and discuss methods for encoding mazes. If there is one thing you take away from this discussion, it is that mazes are graphs and while you may interpret graphs as the cartesian plane, graphs are more generally a "bunch of dots" and "lines between the dots". Pictured: the Cartesian plane, or coordinate system. The space where we project visual representations of 2 (or less) dimension curves as well as our irrational hatred of calculus. They are more formally called vertices and edges. In this paticular case these mazes are trees (graphs with no loops) and therefore:

Eller's Algorithm

This algorithm is extremely efficient. The process is practically instantaneous in the browser environment and it moves in a linear fashion from top to bottom. Eller's algorithm is based on the idea of mapping cells in the maze to sets and then merging these sets together when connections are (randomly) established between disjoint sets.
